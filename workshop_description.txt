[h1]See what your character sees[/h1]

In vanilla PZ, walls and roofs fade out only within roughly 5 tiles of your character, and trees only within a small circle around the character. So the [i]player[/i] behind the screen consistently sees less than the [i]character[/i] has line of sight to: a zombie leaning on the back of a house, a fence corner you can't peek around, a tree directly in front of you on the road. [b]Peek a View[/b] extends those same vanilla fade mechanics further out, so what reaches your screen matches roughly what your character would already perceive.

Same rules as vanilla, just triggered from further out. No X-ray, no see-through walls, no enemy outlines. A faded tree doesn't grant line of sight. Anything behind it still needs to be in your character's line of sight to appear, and a zombie standing partially on a bush or tree tile can still stay hidden. Vanilla's visibility rules still apply: closed doors, curtains, fog of war still hide what they always did.

[h1]What this mod does[/h1]
[list]
[*][b]Wall cutaway[/b]: extends vanilla's ~5-tile cutaway range up to 20 tiles (default 15).
[*][b]Tree fade[/b]: trees in your character's line of sight become translucent up to 25 tiles, in any direction.
[*][b]Two gates[/b]: aim/nimble-stance only (off by default; when on, the mod only runs while you're in that stance) and an in-vehicle speed cutoff (independent km/h thresholds for cutaway and tree fade).
[*]Also fixes a B42 render bug where player-built structures cause nearby vanilla walls to disappear.
[/list]

[h1]Settings[/h1]
Options → Mods → Peek a View, three sections: Global, Wall cutaway, Tree fade. [b]F8[/b] toggles the mod on/off (green/red halo confirms; rebindable in Options → Keybind under [noparse][PeekAView][/noparse]).

Defaults are tuned to show the effect clearly. Reduce ranges, enable aim/nimble-stance only, or tighten driving thresholds to scale it back to your taste.

[h1]Requirements[/h1]
[list]
[*]PZ Build 42.13 or later
[*][url=https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3619862853]ZombieBuddy[/url], one-time install for any Java mod
[/list]

[h1]Compatibility[/h1]
[list]
[*]No known incompatibilities with other Project Zomboid mods at this time.
[*]Singleplayer + multiplayer (client-side rendering only, no server changes).
[*]Localized in EN, DE, FR, ES, RU, PL, PT-BR, IT, TR, CN, KO, JP.
[/list]

[h1]Trade-offs[/h1]
[list]
[*]Visual departure from TIS's intended look. If you prefer the vanilla style, this mod isn't for you.
[*]In dense city blocks, buildings can fade in and out more quickly as you move, which can be distracting at first.
[*]At highway speeds tree fade snaps to its faded state in one frame so you don't drive into opaque crowns.
[*]Per-frame cost is small but not zero. On lower-end hardware, dropping the ranges a few tiles helps.
[/list]
