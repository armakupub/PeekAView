No more peek-a-boo zombies.

In vanilla PZ, walls and roofs only fade out within roughly 5 tiles of your character — so the player behind the screen sees less than the character actually can. A zombie leaning against a house wall or hiding behind a tall fence stays completely invisible to you, even when your character is looking straight at it. [b]Peek a View[/b] closes that gap: houses, view-blocking fences, and now also trees fade from further away, so what reaches your screen matches (mostly) what your character can see.

[h1]What this mod does[/h1]
[list]
[*][b]Wall cutaway[/b] — extends vanilla's ~5-tile cutaway range up to 20 tiles. Configurable.
[*][b]Tree fade[/b] — trees in your character's line of sight become translucent so zombies behind them are visible (not zombies inside them — no X-ray). Independent range slider up to 25 tiles.
[*]Each feature has its own driving-speed gate — turn one off in the car while keeping the other on (e.g. tree fade on at higher speeds for spotting roadside obstacles, wall cutaway off for less screen noise).
[*]Optional nimble-stance-only mode — restrict the whole mod to while you're aiming a weapon.
[*]No see-through walls, no X-ray — same fade mechanics as vanilla, just triggered from further away. You see what the character sees.
[*]Also fixes a B42 render bug where player-built structures cause nearby vanilla walls to disappear.
[/list]

[h1]Settings[/h1]
All options live under Options → Mods → Peek a View, organized into three sections (Global, Wall cutaway, Tree fade). [b]F8[/b] toggles the mod on/off (green/red halo confirms), rebindable under [noparse][PeekAView][/noparse] in Options → Keybind.

[h1]Trade-offs[/h1]
PeekAView changes the visual experience of vanilla PZ on purpose — worth knowing what you're signing up for.
[list]
[*]Not the look TIS intended. The extended ranges are a deliberate departure from the visual style the devs shipped. If you prefer the vanilla look, this mod isn't for you.
[*]In dense city blocks, buildings can fade in and out more quickly as you move — can be distracting.
[*]At highway speeds tree fade snaps fully translucent in one frame so you don't drive into opaque crowns — you can keep tree fade on right up to the 120 km/h max. The driving-speed gate defaults to 100 km/h for that reason.
[*]Per-frame cost is small (caches keep things close to vanilla) but not zero — on lower-end hardware, dropping the ranges a few tiles helps.
[/list]

[h1]Why ZombieBuddy?[/h1]
This is a Java mod. Changing the behavior of a compiled PZ method isn't achievable with a standard Lua mod, and using ZombieBuddy keeps the patch working across minor PZ updates. It's a one-time install — once ZombieBuddy is in your mod list, any ZombieBuddy-based mod can be added without further setup.

[h1]Source & transparency[/h1]
Both PeekAView and ZombieBuddy are MIT-licensed and fully open source. If you'd rather not trust the Workshop binary, you can read every line or build the mod yourself from source. The patches only touch specific PZ rendering classes — no file I/O, no network access, nothing outside PZ's own code.
