title=Peek a View
description=No more peek-a-boo zombies.
description=
description=In vanilla PZ, walls and roofs only fade out within roughly 5 tiles of your character — so the player behind the screen sees less than the character actually can. A zombie leaning against a house wall or hiding behind a tall fence stays completely invisible to you, even when your character is looking straight at it. [b]Peek a View[/b] closes that gap: houses and view-blocking fences fade from further away, so what reaches your screen matches (mostly) what your character can see.
description=
description=[h1]What this mod does[/h1]
description=[list]
description=[*]Extends the cutaway range from vanilla's ~5 tiles up to 20 (configurable).
description=[*]Fixes a B42 render bug where player-built structures cause nearby vanilla walls to disappear.
description=[*]Auto-disables above a configurable driving speed, to keep FPS smooth on lower-end hardware.
description=[*]Optional nimble-stance-only mode.
description=[*]Stays vanilla-style: houses fade by range, walls and fences on the camera-facing side — same mechanics as vanilla, just triggered from further away. No see-through walls, no line-of-sight bypass.
description=[/list]
description=
description=[h1]Options[/h1]
description=Options -> Mods -> Peek a View
description=[list]
description=[*][b]Enable[/b]: master toggle
description=[*][b]Active only in nimble stance[/b]: only while holding right-click to aim a weapon
description=[*][b]Cutaway Range[/b]: 5 to 20 tiles
description=[*][b]PeekAView while driving[/b]: active up to N km/h (0 = always off in a vehicle)
description=[*][b]Fix B42 Wall-Hiding Bug[/b]: on/off
description=[/list]
description=
description=[h1]Hotkey[/h1]
description=[b]F8[/b] toggles the mod on/off (green/red halo confirms). Rebindable under [noparse][PeekAView][/noparse] in Options -> Keybind.
description=
description=[h1]How it works[/h1]
description=Four Java patches, loaded at runtime via ZombieBuddy. The main one enlarges the range PZ uses to decide which walls to fade, plus two filters: one keeps the extra range pointed at actual walls, the other makes sure only things in front of the first wall/window/door from the player fade — so no see-through walls. A second patch cuts out redundant work the engine does once per point of interest, keeping the extended range cheap at 20 tiles. Two small patches fix the B42 wall-hiding bug (player-built stairs/floors making nearby vanilla walls vanish) without touching the normal fading behavior.
description=

description=[h1]Why ZombieBuddy?[/h1]
description=This is a Java mod. Changing the behavior of a compiled PZ method isn't achievable with a standard Lua mod, and using ZombieBuddy keeps the patch working across minor PZ updates. It's a one-time install — once ZombieBuddy is in your mod list, any ZombieBuddy-based mod can be added without further setup. In multiplayer, ZombieBuddy has to be installed before joining the server.

description=[h1]Source & transparency[/h1]
description=Both PeekAView and ZombieBuddy are MIT-licensed and fully open source. If you'd rather not trust the Workshop binary, you can read every line or build the mod yourself from source. The patches only touch specific PZ rendering classes — no file I/O, no network access, nothing outside PZ's own code.

description=[h1]Trade-offs[/h1]
description=PeekAView changes the visual experience of vanilla PZ on purpose — worth knowing what you're signing up for.
description=[list]
description=[*]Not the look TIS intended. The extended cutaway range is a deliberate departure from the visual style the devs shipped. If you prefer the vanilla look, this mod isn't for you.
description=[*]Occasional flickering in dense city blocks with complex geometry. When many buildings stack along the camera axis, a single wall may fade in and out for a frame or two as the player moves.
description=[*]Extra per-frame cost. The larger radius means more work each render pass. On lower-end hardware this is most noticeable while driving — the driving-speed gate exists for exactly this reason and can be dialed down or turned off. Outside the car, dropping the cutaway range by just a few tiles already helps noticeably if you see slowdowns.
description=[/list]
